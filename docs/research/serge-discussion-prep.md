## 1. Remind us both what my job is (30 sec)

## 2. My main idea (say it simply)
> "Instead of one flat label per person, I want each agent to carry a few things at once: their **job**, their **age**, their **household**, their **ethnic group**, and whether they are **displaced**. Then ethnicity and money level change *who they trust* and *how they behave* — they don't make separate models."

The few big points:
- **Young population** — about half are children, so children matter a lot.
- **Mining town** — many people are miners or work around the mine; even some teenagers work.
- **Ethnic groups** (Hema, Lendu, etc.) — matters for **trust and authority**, not for the disease itself.
- **Rich / middle / poor** — matters for access to care, not ethnicity.
- A **few special people** (priests, chiefs, teachers, health workers) are small in number but very important because everyone listens to them.
